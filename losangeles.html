Los Angeles

About

Los Angeles, also known as the "City of Angels," is a vibrant and culturally diverse city in southern California, USA. As the largest city in the state, Los Angeles is a global hub for entertainment, business, and innovation. With roughly 3.9 million residents within the city limits, Los Angeles is the most populated city in California.

Los Angeles was officially incorporated as a municipality on April 4, 1850. Situated in the southern part of the state, Los Angeles serves as the anchor point of the Los Angeles metropolitan area, which includes surrounding cities and communities. Predominately an urban area, Los Angeles does have neighborhoods and areas that can be classified as suburban.

With so many people living in Los Angeles, the average income varies across different neighborhoods and industries. According to the U.S. Census Bureau, the median household income in Los Angeles was around $69,778. The median household income in California is around $84,097 (Census Bureau, 2022).

Ever wondered why they call Los Angeles the "City of Angels"? Los Angeles is Spanish for "The Angels," and the city's full name is "El Pueblo de Nuestra Señora la Reina de los Ángeles del Río de Porciúncula," which translates to "The Town of Our Lady the Queen of the Angels of the Porciúncula River."
